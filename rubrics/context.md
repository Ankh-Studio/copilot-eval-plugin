# Context Provider Evaluation Rubric

## Data Quality (Weight: 0.25)

**Score 4**: High-quality, accurate, and current data with proper validation and cleaning. **Score
3**: Good data quality with minor accuracy or completeness issues. **Score 2**: Basic data quality
with some inaccuracies or outdated information. **Score 1**: Poor data quality that may mislead or
provide incorrect context. **Score 0**: No meaningful data quality controls.

## Relevance (Weight: 0.25)

**Score 4**: Highly relevant context that directly supports intended use cases. **Score 3**: Good
relevance with mostly applicable context information. **Score 2**: Moderately relevant with some
unrelated or redundant information. **Score 1**: Poor relevance with mostly unrelated context.
**Score 0**: No relevant context provided.

## Coverage (Weight: 0.20)

**Score 4**: Comprehensive coverage of domain with all essential topics included. **Score 3**: Good
coverage with most important topics represented. **Score 2**: Basic coverage missing important
concepts or areas. **Score 1**: Limited coverage that doesn't adequately support the domain. **Score
0**: No meaningful coverage achieved.

## Accessibility (Weight: 0.15)

**Score 4**: Easily accessible with efficient retrieval and clear formatting. **Score 3**: Good
accessibility with reasonable retrieval performance. **Score 2**: Basic accessibility with slow or
difficult retrieval. **Score 1**: Poor accessibility that hampers effective use. **Score 0**:
Inaccessible or unusable context.

## Maintainability (Weight: 0.15)

**Score 4**: Well-structured and documented, easy to update and maintain. **Score 3**: Good
maintainability with clear organization and documentation. **Score 2**: Basic maintainability with
some organizational challenges. **Score 1**: Poor maintainability that makes updates difficult.
**Score 0**: No consideration for maintainability.
